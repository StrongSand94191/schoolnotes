The main differences between the federalists and the antifederalists were that the federalists supported the Constitution and a strong central government, while antifederalists didn't support the Constitution and felt that a weak central government would avoid the issues that the colonies faced under British control.

The Articles of Confederation didn't have the power to tax citizens or to create a national army.

The concept of Republican Motherhood was the culture around women in families at the time. It revolved around mothers teaching children proper moral values, like loving one's beautiful country, and being a good citizen.
